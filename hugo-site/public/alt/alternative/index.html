Frequently Asked Questions

What is effective altruism?

Effective altruism is a research field and community dedicated to finding the best ways to help others. It uses evidence and careful reasoning to determine the most effective ways to improve the world. This means not just asking "How can I make a difference?" but "How can I make the biggest difference?"

How can I get involved?

There are many ways to get started! You could attend one of our events, join an introductory reading group, book a free career chat, or start by visiting Giv Effektivt to learn about high-impact giving. We welcome people at every stage of their EA journey.

Where should I donate?

We recommend donating through Giv Effektivt, our Danish sister organization. They follow research from GiveWell and other evaluators to recommend charities that save the most lives per krone donated. You also get Danish tax deductions.

Is EA just about donating money?

Not at all! While effective giving is one important part, EA also encompasses career choice, volunteering, research, and community building. Many people find that choosing the right career is one of the highest-impact decisions they can make. Our career guidance service can help you think through your options.

What is Effective Altruism?

In this short talk, philosopher Will MacAskill explains the core ideas behind effective altruism: using evidence and reason to figure out how to do the most good in the world.

Many of us want to make a difference, but finding the most impactful way to help is not straightforward. EA offers a framework for thinking carefully about where our efforts can go furthest.
