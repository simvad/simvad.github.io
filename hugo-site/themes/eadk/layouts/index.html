About Effective Altruism

How Can We Use Our Time & Money to Help Others the Most?

The effective altruist community believes that when doing good, we should use evidence and reason to guide us. Effective altruists aim to consider all causes and actions, and then act in the way that brings about the greatest positive impact.

Radical Empathy

Moral consideration should extend beyond traditionally recognised groups to encompass a broader range of entities. It emphasizes the importance of identifying and valuing all beings and entities, regardless of their differences from us.

Scope Sensitivity

A willingness to make decisions based on a comprehensive understanding of impact and scale. It involves choosing ways to improve the world based on systematic evaluation rather than personal biases.

Scout Mindset

The search to see the world as it truly is, not as we wish it to be. Consider other views and cognitive biases, and be willing to update our view based on new information.

What is effective altruism?

Many of us aspire to make a meaningful impact in the world. Witnessing suffering, injustice, and death prompts us to want to take action. Yet, finding a tangible solution to these issues, and more importantly, implementing it, is not straightforward.

Effective altruism offers a solution to this challenge. It is a research field dedicated to maximizing the impact of our efforts to aid others. By employing rigorous evidence and thoughtful reasoning, effective altruism seeks to address the question of how we can help others most effectively.
